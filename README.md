# Attention Beeper

Attention Beeper is a lightweight web application that generates **periodic or random beep sounds** to help users maintain focus during work or study sessions.

The application implements a well-studied behavioral technique called **Self-Monitoring of Attention (SMA)**. At each beep, the user briefly checks whether they are focused on the task. If attention has drifted, the beep acts as a cue to redirect attention back to the task.

This method has been used in **behavioral psychology, ADHD interventions, classroom attention training, and productivity research** for decades.

---

# Why It Works

Human attention tends to drift during monotonous or cognitively demanding tasks. Random or periodic auditory cues can interrupt mind-wandering and prompt users to **self-monitor their focus**.

When a beep occurs, the user performs a quick mental check:

> “Am I currently focused on the task?”

If not, they redirect attention immediately.

Over time, this builds **self-regulation and sustained attention**.

This technique is commonly referred to in research as:

- **Self-Monitoring of Attention (SMA)**
- **Cued Self-Monitoring**
- **Auditory Prompt Self-Monitoring**

Historically, early experiments used **cassette tapes with random beeps** (often called *beeper tapes*) to trigger attention checks.

# How to Use

1. Open the web app.
2. Select the interval mode:
   - **Fixed Interval**
   - **Random Interval**
3. Configure the interval settings.
4. Start the session.
5. Each time you hear a beep, quickly ask:

   > “Am I focused on the task right now?”

6. If not, gently redirect attention back to the task.

---

# Recommended Settings

| Mode | Suggested Range |
|-----|-----|
| Fixed | 60–120 seconds |
| Random | 30–180 seconds |
| Deep Work | 60–90 seconds |

Random intervals are often more effective because the brain cannot anticipate the cue.

# Research Background

The Attention Beeper concept is based on decades of research in **behavioral psychology and educational interventions** demonstrating that **self-monitoring with auditory cues increases on-task behavior and attention**.

Below are key research papers related to this technique.

### Early Research

**Glynn, T., Thomas, J., & Shee, S. (1973)**  
*Cued self-monitoring of classroom behavior.*  
One of the earliest demonstrations that prompting students to periodically evaluate their attention significantly improves on-task behavior.

---

**Snider, V. (1987)**  
*Use of Self-Monitoring of Attention with Learning Disabled Students.*  
Students listened to **random beep cues from a tape recorder** and recorded whether they were paying attention. The intervention significantly increased academic productivity and sustained attention.

---

### Reviews and Behavioral Research

**Reid, R. (1996)**  
*Self-Monitoring of Attention: A Review of Research.*  
A comprehensive review concluding that **self-monitoring interventions are among the most effective behavioral techniques for increasing attention and on-task behavior.**

---

**Huisinga, R. (1997)**  
*Self-Monitoring: An Efficient and Effective Intervention.*  
Used intermittent auditory cues at **1–5 minute intervals** to prompt attention checks. Students showed improved classroom engagement and focus.

---

### ADHD and Self-Regulation Research

**Barry, T. & Haraway, D. (2005)**  
*Self-Monitoring and ADHD Classroom Behavior.*  
Self-monitoring techniques improved attention and reduced off-task behavior in students with ADHD.

---

**McDougall, D., Skouge, J., Farrell, C., & Hoff, K. (2006)**  
*Research on Self-Management Techniques Used by Students with Disabilities.*  
A review of 40+ studies concluding that self-monitoring interventions significantly improve attention and academic performance.

---

**Mirnasab, M., & Bonab, B. (2011)**  
*Effects of Self-Monitoring Technique on Inattentive Behaviors of Children with ADHD.*  
Experimental study showing self-monitoring reduces inattentive behavior and improves focus.

---

### Recent Studies

**Alsalamah, A. (2017)**  
*Use of the Self-Monitoring Strategy among Students with ADHD: A Systematic Review.*  
Systematic review confirming strong evidence that self-monitoring interventions improve attention and classroom performance.

---

**Ahmadi et al. (2025)**  
*The Effectiveness of the Self-Monitoring of Attention Educational Program on Concentration and Cognitive Flexibility.*  
Recent research showing that attention self-monitoring improves concentration and cognitive flexibility in students with learning disabilities.
